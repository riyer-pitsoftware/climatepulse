# Conclusion: Climate Change Is a Food Security Problem

The chain holds — partially:

1. **Extreme weather → crop failure:** Strong. Heat stress and drought drive measurable yield loss (CV R² = 0.66).
2. **Crop failure → economic impact:** Moderate. Yield anomalies correlate with price changes (r = -0.32), but price has many drivers beyond weather.

The 2021 drought is not an anomaly — it is a preview. As extreme weather intensifies on the Canadian Prairies, the chain from heat to harvest to household cost will tighten.

ClimatePulse gives us a framework to watch it happen — and the honesty to say where the data stops and the uncertainty begins.
