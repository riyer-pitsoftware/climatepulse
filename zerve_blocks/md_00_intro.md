# ClimatePulse: When the Heat Hits the Harvest

**Thesis:** Extreme Weather → Crop Failure → Economic Impact

In the summer of 2021, a heat dome parked over western Canada. Saskatchewan wheat yields collapsed 24% below the 25-year baseline. Heat stress days more than doubled. Wheat prices surged past $300/tonne.

Can we trace this chain — from weather station to harvest to commodity price — using 25 years of Canadian government data?

**Pipeline:** Three independent data branches feed a unified feature matrix, then an XGBoost model tests whether weather predicts yield — and whether yield loss predicts price spikes.
