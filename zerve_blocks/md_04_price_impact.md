# Price Impact: Yield Loss → Economic Consequences

**Question:** When yields collapse, do commodity prices spike?

**Finding:** Moderate negative correlation (Pearson r = -0.322, p < 0.001, OLS R² = 0.104). Yield anomalies explain ~10% of price variance — statistically significant but not dominant.

**By crop:** Barley and canola show the strongest yield-to-price signal. Wheat, despite being the most visible crop, has a surprisingly weak price correlation.

**Honest assessment:** The weather → yield link is strong (CV R² = 0.66). The yield → price link is real but partial. Climate is one of many price drivers — trade policy, global supply, and storage dynamics also matter. We report what the data shows, not what we hoped to find.
