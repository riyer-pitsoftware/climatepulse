**Climate change is a food security crisis. Here's what 25 years of Canadian data told us.**

For ZerveHack 2026, I built ClimatePulse — a data science project tracing the chain from extreme weather to crop failure to economic impact across the Canadian Prairies.

The signal is real. In 2021, Western Canada's historic drought doubled heat stress days across Saskatchewan. Wheat yields dropped 24% below baseline. Prices surged past $300/tonne. Three provinces, four crops, one brutal pattern: when the climate breaks, the food system breaks with it.

We assembled a 300-row feature matrix spanning ECCC weather records, StatsCan crop yields, and commodity prices, then trained an XGBoost model with SHAP explainability. The model achieves R²=0.68 on normal years — but honestly fails on the 2021 extreme. That gap is the finding: our models are not ready for the tail risks that climate change is making routine.

The price-yield correlation (r=-0.37) confirms the economic link is measurable but complex. This is not a solved problem. It is an urgent one.
